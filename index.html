Introduction

My Name

As mentioned above, I'm Davey Blackstock. Hopefully this should make things clear on the offchance that you assumed that 'Davey Blackstock' was a term similar to 'Preface'. Worry not it is simply my name.

Names are at the same time both simple and incredibly complicated. Simple things about names:

  • They're different for every person so you can tell people apart.
  • A name helps you refer back to a person you met a long time ago.

There are also a lot of complicated things about names which in normal day-to-day life it's best to overlook for fear of being overwhelmed:

  • Multiple people can and do have the same names.
  • Names are a contract between the named person, the person referring to the named individual and wider society (including institutions such as the government, who takes pride in recording peoples names).
  • Names can and do change! The most typical example of this is surnames changing as part of marriage, but there are many more examples of name changes.
  • Names vary so much worldwide. Some cultures will use many middle names, or refer to individuals by their family name instead of their individual name, and many more examples.
